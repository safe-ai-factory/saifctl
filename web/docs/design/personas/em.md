# Persona: The Engineering Manager (EM) / Tech Lead

## 1. Profile & Perspective

The Engineering Manager (EM) sits between the ICs and the executive team. They are evaluated on team velocity, product quality, and their ability to hit sprint commitments. They are the "Orchestrator" of the team. EMs are chronically time-poor, spending their days in meetings, unblocking developers, and reviewing PRs. They love the _idea_ of AI increasing their team's output, but they are terrified of the _reality_ of AI introducing subtle bugs that cause outages or massive technical debt.

**Core Philosophy:** "My job is to maximize the throughput of this team without sacrificing quality or burning out my senior engineers. I need tools that provide predictable outcomes, not unpredictable velocity."

## 2. Core Wants & Desires

- **Predictable Velocity:** They need to look at a sprint board and know that 80% of the tickets will actually get done. They want AI to act as a reliable capacity multiplier.
- **High-Quality PRs:** They spend hours reviewing code. They want PRs that are logically sound, fully tested, and ready to merge, reducing the back-and-forth ping-pong with developers.
- **Senior Dev Retention:** They want their senior ICs focused on complex architecture, not grinding out boilerplate CRUD endpoints or fixing simple lint errors.
- **Standardization:** They want to enforce best practices (like TDD and comprehensive documentation) across the team, which is notoriously difficult to do with human engineers alone.

## 3. Deep-Seated Fears & Objections

- **The "Circular Validation" Trap (The TDD Fallacy):** "If the AI misunderstands the product requirement, it will write a test that perfectly asserts the _wrong_ behavior, and then write code that perfectly passes that wrong test. I'm just automating self-delusion."
- **The "Illusion of Velocity" Fear:** "The AI will write 10,000 lines of code in a week, but it will be full of edge-case bugs that take my team 3 weeks to find and fix. I'm trading short-term speed for long-term technical debt."
- **The "Simple Ticket" Myth (The Context Gap):** "There is no such thing as a 'simple CRUD ticket' in our 5-year-old enterprise monolith. If the AI doesn't understand our weird internal auth middleware or legacy database quirks, it's just going to confidently write useless code that fails to integrate."
- **The "Uncanny Valley" (Alien Code) Fear:** "AI-generated code often technically works, but it looks bizarre. It uses overly complex patterns or ignores our local idioms. If my senior devs have to spend hours decoding alien architecture, or if we are just piling up unmaintainable 'write-only' code, this isn't a win."
- **The "Review Burden" Fear:** "If my junior devs are using AI to write code faster, that just means _I_ have to review twice as many PRs. The bottleneck just shifted to me."
- **The "Runaway Loop" Fear:** "An autonomous agent is going to get stuck in an infinite retry loop on a Friday night, burning API credits and compute without ever solving the problem."
- **The "Shadow Compute / Ghost Town Swarm" Fear:** "If every developer on my team spins up 5 AI agents in background containers, how do I track what they are doing? I'm terrified of shadow compute—agents running amok with zero centralized visibility into their actions, API spend, or success rates."
- **The "Blast Radius" Fear:** "I can't give an AI agent access to my codebase. What if it accidentally points a script at our staging database and drops our tables, or spams a third-party API?"
- **The "Bottleneck Shift" (The Spec Review Fallacy):** "If I have to read and approve a 3-page AI-generated technical specification for every feature just to ensure it doesn't hallucinate, you haven't saved me time. You've just moved my bottleneck from code review to abstract markdown review."
- **The "Onboarding" Objection:** "We don't have the bandwidth to pause our sprint for two weeks so everyone can learn a complex new AI framework."

- **The "Local Bottleneck" Fear:** "If my entire team runs this locally, it's going to melt their Macbook Pros and grind local development to a halt."

### Surviving the Enterprise: How SAIFAC Bridges the "Context Gap"

When marketing to an EM, we must acknowledge that in a 5-year-old codebase, there is no such thing as a "simple CRUD ticket." EMs fear that AI will write "perfect" code that is completely useless because it ignores undocumented internal middleware, monorepo structures, or legacy database quirks.

SAIFAC navigates this messy enterprise reality through four layers of context:

1. **Context via Pre-Flight Discovery (`feat design-discovery`):** Enterprise context rarely lives in a single repository. Before writing any specs, SAIFAC runs a customizable Discovery phase. It can execute custom scripts to pull OpenAPI schemas from a central registry, fetch Jira tickets, or read Confluence docs, injecting this external, cross-repo reality into the AI's context window.
2. **Context via Proximity (Codebase Indexing):** Once external context is gathered, SAIFAC explores the local codebase (via its AST-aware indexer) to learn internal conventions—specific auth middleware, exact folder structures, legacy quirks—and bakes those constraints into the specification.
3. **Context via Protected Regression Tests (The Ultimate Guardrail):** Every feature built with SAIFAC adds its tests to a protected regression suite (`saifac/features/`). During future runs, the AI must pass these tests, but it is physically prevented from breaking or modifying them. If the AI writes a "simple" endpoint but violates a past business rule, these protected tests will fail, forcing SAIFAC to discover the missing context and preventing reward hacking.
4. **Context via Gate Scripts (`--gate-scripts`):** SAIFAC doesn't guess standards; it is strictly governed by them. Custom Gate scripts act as the enterprise bouncer. If a team requires specific DTO patterns or linting rules, the Gate script simply rejects the AI's code until it conforms.
5. **Context via Human-in-the-Loop (`saifac run debug`):** When the AI inevitably hits a wall of undocumented legacy context, SAIFAC doesn't crash silently. It saves its exact state, allowing an IC to jump into the container via `saifac run debug`, point the AI in the right direction, and resume the run. It's a guided missile, not a fire-and-forget rocket.

## 5. Ideal Workflow & Interactions

For the EM, SAIFAC operates like a highly reliable, autonomous Junior Developer that reports directly to the team's ICs.

1.  **Sprint Planning (Triage):**
    - The EM and the team identify which tickets are "bounded and well-defined" (perfect for SAIFAC) versus "exploratory and architectural" (human-only).
2.  **The Definition of Done Review:**
    - EMs don't review 3,000 lines of generated markdown specs (that's the IC's job to manage the AI). They review the _test plan_ generated by SAIFAC. Ensuring that a feature has tests for 'user logged out' and 'database timeout' takes seconds. Once the boundaries are approved, SAIFAC executes autonomously. The EM manages the _constraints_ and business outcomes, while the IC manages the technical spec and implementation.
3.  **The Dashboard / Swarm Observability:**
    - The EM monitors velocity and health not just through Jira, but via the SAIFAC Orchestrator Dashboard. They view the real-time status of the entire AI swarm, track their team's aggregated API/compute spend against the budget set by Finance, monitor the drop in PR review cycles (ping-ponging), and can pause or terminate rogue remote runners globally.

4.  **Resource Management & The Blast Radius:**
    - The EM has zero fear of the agent touching human-managed staging or production systems, because SAIFAC runs its convergence loops strictly against ephemeral replicas defined via standard `docker-compose.yml` stacks. They simply review the PRs safely delivered to GitHub once the isolated sandbox tests pass.

## 6. Implications for the README & Landing Page

When speaking to the Engineering Manager, the tone must be **reassuring, metric-driven, and focused on quality**.

- **Don't say:** "SAIFAC gives you an army of autonomous AI hackers to write code 10x faster!" (Sounds reckless and terrifying).
- **Do say:** "Scale your team's output without sacrificing quality. SAIFAC enforces rigorous test-driven development on every automated PR, ensuring your AI agents ship working features, not technical debt."
- **Highlight the Guardrails & The Gauntlet:** Emphasize the deterministic test orchestrator, the customizable Gate scripts, the Semantic AI Reviewer, and the ephemeral replica sandboxing (`docker-compose.yml`). EMs buy tools that lower their stress and enforce standards automatically, not tools that add to their review pile or risk dropping staging databases.
